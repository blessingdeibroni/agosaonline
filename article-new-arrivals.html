Onboarding

New Students Arrive for the 2025/26 Academic Year

By AGOSA Board · September 2025 · 3 min read

This November, AGOSA welcomed a fresh wave of Ghanaian scholarship students arriving across Algiers, Constantine,Oran and more to begin the 2025/26 academic year. For many, it was their first time setting foot in Algeria and the AGOSA welfare team made sure no one faced that first week alone.

Volunteers from the New Arrivals Program coordinated airport pickups at every major arrival point, helped new students secure temporary housing, and distributed welcome packs containing SIM cards, basic groceries, foods and orientation guides translated for ease of understanding.

A smoother transition

Beyond logistics, senior students were paired with new arrivals through the Peer Mentorship program, offering guidance on course registration, navigating French and Arabic-medium coursework, and settling into Algerian university life.

"Landing in a new country alone is daunting, but having someone waiting for you at the airport who speaks your language and understands what you're going through — that changes everything," said one new arrival from the Oran chapter.

Looking ahead

The board says onboarding support will continue throughout the semester, with check-ins planned at the one-month and three-month marks to ensure every new member is settling in academically and socially.

AGOSA extends a warm welcome to all new Ghanaian scholars beginning their journey in Algeria this year.
