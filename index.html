Who We Are

Far from home.
Never alone.

AGOSA was born from a simple truth: navigating a foreign country, a new language, and a rigorous academic system is hard enough — but it's far easier together. Founded in 2006 by a determined group of Ghanaian scholarship students in Algiers, we've grown into a national community representing students across Algeria's finest universities.

From engineering labs in Constantine to medical halls in Oran, every Ghanaian scholar on Algerian soil is part of this family.

Our Journey

A Timeline of
Milestones

2006

AGOSA Founded

A small group of Ghanaian scholarship students in Algiers establish the association to support new arrivals and advocate for members.

2010

Embassy Recognition

AGOSA is formally recognized by the Ghana Embassy in Algiers as the official student liaison body.

2014

Chapters Expand

Constantine and Oran chapters officially open, extending AGOSA's reach to universities across Algeria.

2018

Emergency Support Fund

Members collectively establish a welfare fund to assist students during medical or financial hardship.

2025

2,500+ Members

AGOSA reaches a landmark membership milestone, now representing Ghanaian scholars across 30+ Algerian universities.
